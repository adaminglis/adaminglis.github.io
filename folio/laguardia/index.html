TechHire – Open Code is designed to close the tech gap and benefit the middle class. TechHire is was funded by the Department of Labor and an Obama Administration grant. It is a partnership between LaGuardia Community College, The Software Guild, Udacity, General Assembly, and Uncubed.


Project outline


Re-imagine brand identity, social media strategy and student recruitment touchpoints.

Timeline: 5 Weeks

Deliverables: Social media analysis, design system, re-branded identity, competitive/comparative analysis, user research, and personas, journey maps, user flows, site maps, sketches, wireframes and a clickable prototype for mobile and web.

Conducted: Design sprints, stakeholder interviews, competitive/comparative analysis, usability testing.

Design tools:Figma, miro, canva, pencil and paper.

Research question

How might we make the TechHire - Open Code brand more recognizable, consistent, and reach a greater pool of potential applicants?

The problem

TechHire - Open Code has high-quality, free education for marginalized and underemployed young people—but it is coupled with an inconsistent visual identity and is not reaching it's full potential for talent recruitment.

Solution

  • Develop a clear social media identity and strategy that is organic and targeted.

  • Create a visual identity and design system that is scalable and simple for current and futute print and digital marketing activities.


Competitive analysis and generative research

Background

LaGuardia's Tech Hire - OpenCode is a technology training bootcamp for young people aged 17-29. It is specifically geared towards marginalized groups, people who are underemployed, and those who are underrepresented in the tech sector.

Before the COVID-19 pandemic, classes were held in-person at LaGuardia Community Colledge and online. They have since moved completely online.

The TechHire initiative

"The TechHire initiative was a $150 million grant program of the Obama Administration aimed at getting more people from less advantaged backgrounds in the doors of coding programs or on other technology career paths.

Some of the 2016 TechHire grants were aimed at coding bootcamps, the intense, short-term – several months maybe – training in writing computer code or other similar high-tech skills because, while the bootcamps were offering great, industry-aligned training from subject experts, many of these programs charged up-front tuition. And tuition at coding camps is not covered by federal student grants or aid."

- (Forbes 2019)

Instagram Posts

TechHire - OpenCode were using Instagram to recruit and engage with new candidates, alumni, and employers too. The post sequence needed a stronger visual identity. One that was more on-brand with the image or identity that TechHire were seeking to attract with its tech training courses.

The program needed a veriety of post formats that were recognizable, clear, scalable and easy for marketing staff to recreate over and over. I created templates using Figma and sourced a variety of stock imagery and iconography that could be used, remixed or added to.

I did a content analysis of their existing Instagram feed identified some existing post types, and explored a few new ideas.

Instagram post types:

  • Showcasing emerging talent and alumni
  • Calls to action for upcoming information sessions and courses
  • Inspirational, motivational, and relatable quotes; tech-memes
  • Photography from events, classes and behind the scenes
